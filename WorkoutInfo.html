Workout Cheatsheet

We recommend at least 30 minutes of exercise daily!

Recommended goals: Running is the perfect exercise for losing weight and improving your endurance.
Average calories burned: Average of 350 calories burned per hour of running!
Tips for beginners: Improving your running form can help you run faster, more efficiently and comfortably, and with less stress on your body and reduced injury risk. Proper running form reduces your risk of fatigue and ensures that you are getting the most out of your run. Follow these tips to work on perfecting your form.
  1. Look Ahead
  2. Keep Hands at Your Waist
  3. Relax Your Hands
  4. Check Your Posture
  5. Relax Your Shoulders
  6. Keep Your Arms at Your Sides
  7. Rotate Your Arms From the Shoulder
  8. Don't Bounce

Average calories burned: Average of 285 calories burned per hour of walking!
Tips for beginners: Here are 7 walking tips for beginners that will make sure you get the most out of your walks.
  1. Wear good walking shoes
  2. Maintain good posture
  3. Use your arms
  4. Use a heel-to-toe stride
  5. Pay attention to your halfway point
  6. Be realistic about your current endurance level
  7. Increase your distance gradually

Recommended goals: Doing situps is a great way to build strong muscles!
Average calories burned: Average of 400 calories burned per hour of doing situps!
Tips for beginners: To perform the perfect sit up keep your feet, hips and knees aligned with knees bent and feet flat on the floor, and exhale on your way up and inhale as you return to the start position.
  1. Perform the exercise slowly or in a controlled manner
  2. Don't use your arms to pull up your head
  3. Give your core time to recover.

Recommended goals: Dumbbell exercises are great ways to build strong muscles!
Average calories burned: Average of 200 calories burned per hour of doing dumbbell exercises!
Tips for beginners: The Dumbbell Alternating Bicep Curl is one of the most popular dumbbell exercises out there, as it trains the muscles that help you bend the arm at the elbow.
  1. Find two dumbbells of the same weight
  2. Stand with soft knees (don't lock your knees) and feet shoulder-width apart.
  3. Holding a dumbbell in each hand and facing your palms forward, your elbows should stay locked at your side throughout the exercise.
  4. Starting with your weaker arm, bend your elbow to bring the dumbbell up toward your shoulder. Pause for one second, then return to start.
  5. Repeat on the other arm, then continue in an alternating fashion until you have performed all repetitions on each side.
  6. Keep your stomach muscles tight throughout the exercise, and don't forget to breathe out during the upward movement and inhale during the downward movement.
  7. Try about three sets of 10-15 repetitions on each side.
